Minimal hide snippet 
<!-- 1) Inject gvm.js SDK -->
<script src="https://esm.sh/@wdft/gvm-sdk@latest/gvm.js" type="module"></script>

<!-- 2) Initialize gvm on body -->
<body data-gvm data-gvm-tenant="your_tenant" data-gvm-env="qa" data-gvm-currency="PLN">
  <!-- 3) Declare payment template (modal) -->
  <template data-gvm-template="payment" data-gvm-template-inject-to="body">
    <dialog open><button data-gvm-bind-send-sms>Open SMS</button></dialog>
  </template>

  <!-- 3b) Declare hide paywall template -->
  <template data-gvm-template="paywall" data-gvm-template-inject-to="#article">
    <button data-gvm-bind-pay>Unlock for <span data-gvm-bind-price></span> <span data-gvm-bind-currency></span></button>
  </template>

  <!-- 4) Declare payment item/content -->
  <div id="article" data-gvm-reference="article-42" data-gvm-price="0.75"
       data-gvm-template-name="payment" data-gvm-hide-template-name="paywall"
       data-gvm-hide-strategy="hide" data-gvm-hide-sections="6">...</div>
</body>

The gvm.js hide strategy lets you launch paid article previews without changing your existing CMS rendering pipeline.

On load, selected sections are hidden and replaced with a clear paywall prompt. Readers who convert get immediate access in the same reading session.

This approach is intentionally lightweight and conversion-focused, making it useful for pilots, campaign pages, and market validation.

You can activate it conditionally by device, campaign source, or experiment bucket to measure impact before broad rollout.

The strategy combines two templates: one for payment and one for the locked-content panel shown in the article body.

The next paragraphs represent premium sections. They are visible only after a successful commitment.

In production, many publishers start with a subset of premium stories and expand coverage based on conversion and retention performance.

For controlled rollouts, you can add conditions like data-gvm-cond="utm_source == 'testing-gvm' AND device matchMedia 'max-width:768px,landscape:portrait'" to target only specific audiences.

Keep window.wdftDebug = true; enabled during QA to verify initialization, bindings, and commitment state transitions.

For rapid sandbox testing, use data-gvm-env="demo" in the body. You can refine copy and layout before connecting production settlement paths.

When you are ready for stricter access control, move to redirect or proxy-backed patterns while reusing the same payment template concepts.

Returning users are handled automatically. Existing valid commitments for the same reference are treated as duplicates, which lowers repeat friction.
